3. Ethical Dilemma

Scenario: Your organisation develops an AI-driven digital marketing system that collects user data, analyses behaviour, and delivers targeted advertising. During testing, you discover that the system lacks clear user consent, collects excessive personal data, and may influence users in non-transparent ways. Despite these risks, management insists on releasing the system to maximise profit.
Privacy

The system collects and processes personal data without clear and informed user consent, increasing the risk of misuse.

Security

Weak safeguards may expose sensitive user data to breaches, unauthorised access, or cyberattacks.

Manipulation

AI-driven targeting may influence user decisions unfairly, especially for vulnerable individuals.

Trust

Deploying such a system can damage public trust in both the organisation and digital technologies.

AI can improve efficiency, reduce repetitive work, and support better decision-making.

Outcome: Businesses become faster and more efficient. Data-driven decisions improve services, but over-reliance may reduce human oversight.

Outcome: Innovation increases across industries. Problems are solved more effectively, leading to economic growth and improved technology.

Opposite Opinion to Yours

10. What Should I Do? + Scenario Outcomes

This section combines practical action steps with possible outcomes for each decision.

Step 1: Test the AI system carefully and identify privacy, security, and fairness problems.
Step 2: Record evidence of the risks, such as unclear consent, excessive tracking, or possible bias.
Step 3: Explain the problems to management in a clear and professional way.
Step 4: Compare possible solutions and think about the outcome of each one.
Personal Solution 1: Launch the system with no changes
  • Follow the manager’s instructions
  • Release the AI system immediately
  • Do not raise further objections
Step 1: Approve the unsafe system.
Step 2: Allow the company to launch quickly.
Step 3: Deal with problems only after users are affected.

Scenario outcome: The company may gain short-term profit, but users could experience privacy violations, loss of trust, and harm from unfair or manipulative targeting. Legal and ethical consequences may follow.

Personal Solution 2: Report the issue and suggest fixes
  • Inform management of the risks
  • Recommend clearer user consent
  • Reduce invasive data collection
  • Strengthen security and fairness checks
Step 1: Present the ethical and technical risks.
Step 2: Suggest concrete improvements.
Step 3: Support a safer redesign before release.

Scenario outcome: The release may be delayed, but the final system becomes safer, more transparent, and more ethical. This improves public trust and reduces the chance of legal or reputational damage.

Personal Solution 3: Escalate the concern and refuse unsafe deployment
  • Raise the issue to senior leadership or ethics officers
  • Refuse to approve the unsafe system
  • Protect users even if there is pressure from management
Step 1: Formally document the risks.
Step 2: Escalate the concern beyond the direct manager.
Step 3: Refuse deployment until the system meets ethical and safety standards.

Scenario outcome: This may create workplace conflict, but it best protects users, upholds professional ethics, and prevents greater long-term harm. It also demonstrates integrity and responsibility as a data scientist.

Best choice: Personal Solution 3 is the strongest option because it protects user privacy, follows ethical principles, and prioritises long-term trust over short-term profit.
